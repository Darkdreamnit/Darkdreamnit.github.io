Rv Parks in Gulf Shores: Ultimate Guide.

The Gulf Shores has many different RV parks to offer with its high 80° and 90° summers. Located in Alabama, the Gulf Shores is also known to have warm winters that allow for anyone to still go fishing on its waters or take a timeout at the beach. With many attractions like the Gulf Coast Zoo and the Bon Secour National Wildlife Refuge, you will not be disappointed by taking a vacation in this location. It does not matter if you own an RV or rent one, you will want to visit one or more of the available campgrounds in this location.

  1. Fort Morgan RV Park

    2. Location: 10397 2nd St, GULF SHORES, AL 36542

    The Fort Morgan RV Park has a total of 34 sites spread across six acres and is ran by Rob and Sue Ledlow. Facilities have been added in 2013 such as bathhouse which has free laundry and a community room. There are 30 and 50 amp electricity hookups along with water, cable TV, sewer and there is also wireless internet available throughout the park. All visitors are advised to try their best to check in before 9 pm as this park gets dark at night.

  2. Sugar Sands RV Resort

    3. Location: 5343 Roscoe Rd, Gulf Shores, AL 36542.

    The Sugar Sands RV Resort has a total of 82 sites and includes 5 tiny houses which you can rent. Parking pads measure 22′ x 60′ and are all made with concrete which makes it easy for setting up outside. The early 20th-century architecture will for sure make you feel like you back in the past where times were simpler. You will get this feeling but you will also be able to enjoy the many amenities that we at times cannot seem to live without.

    All 82 sites at the Sugar Sands RV Resort offer full hookups which provide 30/50 amp power, WiFi, sewer/water, and a picnic table. You can choose any of the 82 sites and be confident that it will be well maintained and clean. You will not have to worry about the size of your rig as each site measures in at 22' x 60'. This large of a site will accommodate even the largest rig out there.

  3. Doc’s RV Park

    4. Location: 17595 AL-180, Gulf Shores, AL 36542.

    The Doc’s RV Park is o ly three miles away from some of the best beaches in Gulf Shores and is a safe place to stay as they strictly enforce a 5 mph speed limit to protect everyone in the area. The staff is available throughout the whole day even though there are set times for operation. With free Wi-Fi around the park, you can stay connected with the outside world at all times.

  4. Southport Campground

    5. Location: 108 Waterway W Blvd, Gulf Shores, AL, 36542.

    Even though this campground does not have some amenities like a swimming pool or any type of hiking trail, there are two clubhouses that you can reserve for any type of large group. If you are an angler then you will enjoy the fishing pier that is located inside the campground. For all those who like playing horseshoe, there is a horseshoe court for the whole family. There are also dinners for guests and weekly bingo games.

  5. Gulf State Park

    6. Location: 20115 State Park Rd, Gulf Shores, AL 36542.

    There are roughly two miles of beaches, a very spacious campground with a brand new Conference Center, and a Lodge to greet you on your arrival at the Gulf State Park. The gorgeous white sand and surging surf make for a good day at the beach where there are seagulls overhead and many activities to take part in. People who come here usually return as they have enjoyed all of their previous visits.

    Take the whole family paddling and swimming in Lake Shelby or to the nature center. The nature center is open daily from 9 a.m to 4 p.m and has a live museum of animals and plants that are native to the Gulf Coast Area. Kids can meet and greet animals face to face and see plants they might never see anywhere else in the world. The nature center aims to inspire everyone to take better care of the environment by giving nature walks, pier walks, beach walks, and nature time.

    The Gulf State Park offers 496 full-hookup sites with paved pads and a total of 11 primitive campsites. With running water, outdoor showers, and rustic bathrooms close-by, you will always feel like you are home away from home.
