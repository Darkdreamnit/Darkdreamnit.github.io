WHAT IS A BUTYL SEAL TAPE? Full Guide

This type of tape is an artificial, rubber, pressure-sensitive adhesive (PSA) tape created in the laboratory instead of being made from rubber tree resins that is found in nature. Rubber adhesives are really sticky and sticks to a lot of surface areas very quickly. The high level of versatility and adhesion of butyl tape will always make it a great option for applications that require bonding where there might be some sort of moisture present. Butyl Tape is also great for surface areas to be bonded that are non-porous and smooth like metal or glass.

If you are trying to find a trusted, durable adhesive that does fast repair work in a jiffy, absolutely nothing beats the strength and versatility of the butyl tape. It does an outstanding job of supplying an air-tight and mess-free sealing for boats, EDPM Rubber roofing patching, windows, RVs, and even glass.

Where can you use butyl tape?

  • Rvs

    • It is always best when you have an rv and know how to do certain DIY projects. Butyl tape will make your life much easier than using regular putty type tapes. This type of tape has been getting more and more popular among rvers.

  • Vehicles

    • Use these to stop certain leaks you might have in your vehicle. This is usually only used as a temporary fix until you get the time to do the necessary fixes needed.

  • Household

    • Due to the waterproof nature of butyl tape, it is a great choice when it comes to repairing certain items in a household. This tape can be use to stop the possibility of leakage in areas such as roofing, ventilation systems, joints and pipes, and many other areas.

  • Window Sealing

    • Many people are using butyl tape for window sealing as they find it easy to use.

Pros & Cons of Butyl Tape

Pros
  1. Much cheaper than silicone adhesives or acrylic adhesives.
  2. Bonds easily due to the high degree of adhesion.
  3. Less temperature sensitive than its alternatives.
  4. Higher UVA resistance than natural rubber adhesive type products.
  5. Item is airtight and very watertight.
  6. Butyl tape is very flexible and able to withstand certain contraction and expansion.
Cons
  1. Does not easily stick to rough or uneven surfaces.
  2. Not fully immune to extra high temperatures.
  3. This is a solvent-type tape and will break down when exposed to gasoline, mineral oil, and other types of solvents.

Surface Compatibility Of Butyl Tape

The butyl tape adheres best to smooth surfaces. Here are some of the best surfaces to use butyl tape.

  • Glass
  • Plastic
  • Wood
  • Rubber Roofing
  • Galvanized Steel
  • Aluminum

How to safely use BUTYL SEAL TAPE

  1. Put your butyl tape in the freezer for about 3 hours This makes it much easier to handle.
  2. Clean the working surface you are going to use. Make sure the surface is dry.
  3. You can then easily peel the tape from its roll. Do not touch the adhesive side of the tape.
  4. Place the tape on the surface ypu are trying to repair. When you do this, ensure that the tape is fully flush with the surface. There should be no gaps between the surface and butyl tape.
  5. You can use more layers of tape in the same area to make a more stable seal.
  6. Trim any excess tape with a knife or scissor.
  7. Give the tape enough time to cure by allowing it to dry for about 2 to 4 hours.

Does butyl tape come in only one size?

Butyl tape is a great item to have in many situations. Some situations might require a small piece of butyl tape while some jobs might require an extra long piece. Butyl tape usually comes on a roll just like any other type of tape. This means you can cut it into the different sizes you might want.
