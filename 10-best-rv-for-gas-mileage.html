10 Best Rvs for gas mileage

One of the most important questions to ask a salesperson when you are in the market to purchase any type of vehicle would be "how many miles does it get per gallon?". Any vehicle with a low mpg count will be costly to the owner in the long run. This same principle applies to purchasing any type of recreational vehicle. So, do not be afraid to ask how many mpg your rv will get.

Which Factors Affect RV Gas Mileage?

There are a few different factors that affects rv gas mileage.

  • Speed

    • It does not matter which type of gas vehicle you drive, the speed at which you travel will determine how many miles you get per gallon. Other factors of speed will have to be taken into consideration also. Factors such as how fast you got up to the optimal speed or how slow you got up to that optimal speed. The optimal speed for the best gas mileage is at around 55-65 MPH. Any more than that speed, gas mileage will decrease.

  • Wind

    • Wind speed has an effect on gas mileage in the fact that travelling head-on into high winds will cut your gas mileage down. It might not be by much, but it will definitely be lower than it should be. Vice Versa, if you have the wind blowing in the direction you're travelling.

  • Ethanol Content

    • Ethanol Content plays a major roll in the amount of miles you get per gallon.

  • Weight

    • The lighter your rv is the better the gas mileage will be. The lighter the rv you purchase, the more gear you will be able to tow or carry without affecting your gas mileage by too much.

  • Grade

    • Just like many different factors on this list, grade has a lot to do with getting the best gas mileage out of your rv. Any vehicle

  • Fuel Type

    • I know this is a blog post about gas mileage, but i have to put it in here that diesel vehicles get better miles per gallon than a gas vehicle. Diesel engines provide 10-15% more energy than its gas counterpart as well as providing more low-end torque. If a gas vehicle uses 1 gallon of fuel for every 20 miles traveled, its diesel counter-part will go 20-30% more miles on the same amount of fuel.

How to get the best fuel mileage in your rv

  • Change your air filters frequently

    • Something that seems so simple as changing your air filter on a schedule plays a big role in the mileage you get out of your rv. Rule of thumb when changing your engine oil and oil filter is to check to see if your air filter has gotten less efficient in any way. This could be by inspecting to see if there are any foreign objects restricting airflow through it. Other factors that will affect the effectiveness of your filter are how often you drive within the city or on the highway, or the type of climate you live in. The type of fuel you use will also be a factor in how often you have to change or check your filter.

    Diesel vehicles are usually fitted with an “Air Restriction Indicator”. This indicator alerts the driver whenever it is time to replace the filter.

  • Tire Pressure

    • There is a tire pressure recommendation on the wall of all tires. This recommended tire pressure is there for us to use as a guideline to get the best performance out of our tires as much as possible.
