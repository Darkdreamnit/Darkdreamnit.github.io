Generators for camping: Ultimate Guide.

Whenever we go out camping, it is always a good idea to take a backup source of electricity. Here i will list 5 generators that i think everyone should consider. One Generator on the list is my favorite because i personally own one and have also upgraded the gas tank to be able to run it for longer hours.

1. Westinghouse WGen7500


The Westinhouse WGen7500 a portable gas powered generator that is great for emergency backup energy around the campground. It comes equipped with a Push Button start and also a wireless remote. This allows you to have a quick start when needed. The equipped 420cc Westinghouse OHV 4-Cycle Engine is designed to give an easier start and provides great fuel-efficiency. This engine has cast Iron sleeves which helps to extend its life. You will not have to worry about adding oil before the due date as this engine has low oil consumption which helps prevent engine damage.

To feed fuel to the 420cc engine, this generator is equipped with a 6.6-Gallon Fuel Tank and a Fuel Guage so you know exactly when you will need to add fuel. With a full tank of fuel, you can run this generator for roughly 10 hours at a 50% load. It is equipped with Automatic Voltage Regulator and a Circuit Breaker which is needed to offer Protection against harmful voltage surges.

One simple item that comes along with this generator is the flaps that protect the outlets from debris or moisture when they are not in use. And who coukld overlook the 10" solid wheel which make this generator more than portable and not prone to punctures. there is also a single Fold-Down handle for easy storage.

2. Honda EU2200i

This generator isn't the most powerful on the list, but it deserves to be on this list because of the ease of using it and it's undeniable portability. It delivers 2200Watts which is almost enough power to run every item in your rv. It is equipped with the GXR120 engine that produce 20% more power than the previous version. It has been built to be more quiet and smooth. The bigger oil filter that is fitted to this generator allows for easier and cleaner oil changes.

The highest quality magnets are used in this unit to produce very high quality inverter power without having to make any sacrifices in the weight department. When it comes to reliability, Honda is one of those companies that ensures the best. With that being said, with any purchase of one of these generators, you will be given awarranty from top to bottom for 3 years.

This generator comes in at a price of $1009
3. Champion 3800-Watt Dual Fuel RV Ready Portable Generator with Electric Start

The Champion 3800-Watt Dual Fuel RV Ready Portable Generator with Electric Start is versatile in that it is capable of running on gas or propane without any modification to the engine. It carries a 4-Stroke engine which outputs 4750 starting watts and 3000 running watts. This generator will run for 9 hours on a full tank of gas and for 10.5 hours if you decide to use 20lb propane tank. The 224cc Champion engine comes with the easy push-button start which is way easier than using the pull cord.

All outlets provided on this generator are protected by Volt Guard which protects all appliances from power surges.

This generator comes in at a price of $646.99
4. GOAL ZERO YETI 400 POWER STATION + BOULDER 50 SOLAR KIT

This list wouldn't be fully taken care of if it didn't have a solar powered generator. It definately isn't the most powerful on this list but what it lacks in power, it makes up for in size and weight. It comes with 400wh, 33Ah(12V) battery capacity. This is enough to power many of your mobile devices like cellphones, tablets, laptops and many other small devices. There are numerous ports available which include:

  • Two AC Outlets
  • 12V output
  • Two USB Ports
  • 6mm port: 12V, up to 10A (120W max)
  • Power Pole Chaining Port: 12V, up to 33A (400W max)
  • Charging Port (input, 8mm, blue, circle): 14-29V, up to 10A (120W max)

This generator has many different ways in which you can charge the included battery:

  • Wall Charger (72W): 5 Hours
  • Car Charger (30W): 13 Hours
  • Nomad 20: 31-62 Hours
  • Nomad 50: 12-24 Hours
  • Boulder 50: 12-24 Hours
  • Boulder 100: 6-12 Hours
  • Boulder 100 Briefcase: 6-12 Hours
  • Nomad 100: 6-12 Hours
  • Boulder 200 Solar Panel: 3-6 Hours

There are two AC outlets as stated above but this can be extended by up to two more if you use a power inverter in the 12V ac outlet.

This generator comes in at a price of $574.95
5. Powerhorse Portable Generator - 4000 Surge Watts, 3100 Rated Watts

The PowerHorse 4000 is my favourite as i have personally owned one for over 3 years. The photo on the left is of a brand new item and the one on the rightr is of my modified item with an upgraded fuel tank. The original fuel tank is a 3.9 Gallon tank but i upgraded mine to an 8 Gallon. The original 3.9 Gallon tank is able to run the generator for 10 Hours at a 50% load. I have never tried running it for more than 14 Hours with my upgraded tank just yet but i know the 10 Hour mark is just about right on the original tank.

The major plus for this genertator are the 8 120Volt outlets that it has along with 1 120V/240V 30 amp outlet. With that many outlets, you can have many different tools running at a time you might want to invest in some extension cords.

Startup
The Powerhorse portable generator uses a recoil system to start its engine.

Here are the steps to be taken to start the generator.

  1. Check to see that the generator is on a level surface.
  2. Check the engine oil and the fuel.
  3. Check and see that there is no load connected to the generator.
  4. Move the fuel valve to the On position.
  5. Turn the Engine switch to the On position.
  6. Move the Choke to the On position.
  7. Move the throttle lever about 1/3 to the Fast position.
  8. Pull the recoil handle.
  9. If the Choke was moved to the On position, move it slowly to the Off position while the engine warms up.

Shut down

  1. Move the throttle lever to the Slow position and allow the engine to run for a couple of minutes.
  2. Turn the Engine switch to the Off position.
  3. Turn the fuel valve to the Off position

This generator comes in at a price of $450
